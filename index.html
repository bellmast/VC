Venture Capital

  • 1. How VC affects companies
    • A) Non-cash benefits
      • 1) VC-backed companies professionalize their businesses earlier than other, similar companies (e.g., by adopting HR departments, product timelines, etc.).
        • i) Hellmann and Puri (2000) established this fact by looking at a sample of 200 firms in the Minneapolis area. They used semi-structured surveys and found that 90% of VC-backed firms had professionalized along these measures by month 6, as compared to 60% of similar companies using other finance.
    • B) Drivers of impact
      • 1) VCs can have a greater impact if they invest earlier
        • i) Lee et al. (2001) found this using a discontinuity regression on 200 VC backed firms, sourced from Thomson-Reuters. While no one has yet replicated their results, their methodology is extremely sound and their conclusions compelling.
      • 2) VCs can have a greater impact if they are geographically close to the company
        • i) Lee et al. (2001) also found this in their paper, which, as above, used a discontinuity regression on 20 VC backed firms, sourced from Thomson-Reuters. While no one has yet replicated their results, their methodology is extremely sound and their conclusions compelling
    • C) Negative effects
